Let's Learn! Information to Get Started An audit is defined as an official inspection, and in this activity, you will conduct an audit of your own home! This audit will test the efficiency--the state or condition of achieving maximum productivity--of the appliances, doors, and windows in your home. Check this out! It’s not safe, without proper training, to touch gas appliances, including your furnace or hot water heater, so we’ll leave that to the professionals. You can investigate the efficiency of your windows and doors, however. How much does the air temperature change when you move from the middle of a room to the area immediately around your windows and doors? Do you notice a temperature change? Can you feel a draft inside on a windy day? Take notes on this activity sheet.

How many inefficient light bulbs do you have in your home? Do you know where they are? According to the Department of Energy, incandescent light bulbs are the least efficient form of light bulb in homes. They are many different types, and these bulbs typically have shorter lives. Record these on your activity sheet.

How can I do more?

Next, talk to your parents about scheduling a home efficiency audit with one of our partners, Fort Collins Utilities or Clearwater Exteriors. For a home energy efficiency audit, go to https://www.fcgov.com/utilities/epicHomes
