Reduce That Energy Use

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

According to the Department of Energy, by moving your air conditioning unit out of direct heat and humidity you can drastically improve its energy efficiency. Heating & cooling is one of the greatest users of energy throughout the United States. Two out of three American homes have an air conditioning unit, and within these homes, 6% of this energy use is devoted purely to running an air conditioner.


Air conditioning units and evaporative coolers work like refrigerators: they transfer heat from the interior of the home to the warm outdoor environment. Because an excessive amount of humidity decreases the body’s ability to perspire and cool itself down, many air conditioning units also reduce the amount of moisture that’s in the air. Because of the way that air conditioning units work, they are much less efficient in an area that has a high humidity and high heat.


A quick and easy way to help your thermostat run more efficiently is to turn on a bathroom fan while showering or taking a bath. This will reduce the humidity in your home and help your AC unit to use far less energy. If possible and safe, you may also want to consider moving your AC unit out of direct heat and into a space in which your home stays at a moderate temperature year round.

Find the main AC unit in your home. Is it in an efficient spot, out of direct heat and high humidity? Is there another space within your home that you could relocate your air conditioning unit to that may help your family save money?


Lead the discussion in your home about air conditioning unit efficiency and the impact that the position of your unit can have on your energy costs and carbon footprint. Consider changing household habits or moving your unit to make your home more efficient.
