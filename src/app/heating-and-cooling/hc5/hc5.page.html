Wash it Smarter in Cold Water

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

Did you know that roughly 75% of the energy used when washing a load of laundry goes into heating the water? Changing your washer temperature settings from 75 °F to 60 °F can save your household $60 or more each year! Many people wash their clothes in warm or hot water because it is known to make the chemical reactions in many detergents work more quickly and effectively. Some detergents are specially formulated for use in cold water, however, and work just as effectively in your more environmentally sustainable cold wash.

Take the Pledge Wash it Smarter in Cold Water

What does all this mean? Wash your clothes in cold water! Lead the conversation in your home about making the switch from warm to cold water in every laundry load.


If you or your parents are not sure about making the change, run a test! Wash one load of laundry in a cold-water cycle to see the results. Then compare it to one load washed in warm or hot water. This will help you know what the future of laundry looks like in your home.
