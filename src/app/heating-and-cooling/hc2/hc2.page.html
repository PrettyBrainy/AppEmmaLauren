Chill Out

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

An electric space heater can use more than 45 watts of energy every hour it is turned on. That’s about $5 per hour in energy costs AND 45 extra watts of energy burned, which greatly increases your carbon emissions! If your family relies on a space heater to keep your home warm, these costs can quickly add up. Electric space heaters are also prone to overwhelming your electric system and tripping breakers — the system in charge of shutting off your entire electrical system in case of an electricity overload.


Instead of using a space heater (or to use one less), do these things instead:


Dress for the Weather: wear an extra sweater, warm socks, and a scarf.


Keep Out Drafts: Make your house draft free. We can help you with this! Go to the “Plug Loads” peak and click on the pledge titled “Airtight and Alright.” The pledge walks you through your own preliminary home efficiency audit!

Take the Pledge Chill Out

Take initiative and lead the conversation with your parents about the consequences of owning a space heater and whether or not this appliance is necessary in your home. Discuss alternatives and ask your parents to consider donating or recycling your space heater to decrease your carbon footprint.


When recycling your space heater (if this applies to you) make sure to do it responsibly. Go to Earth911 to learn where you can safely recycle your space heater.
