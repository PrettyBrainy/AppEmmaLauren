Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

In 2017 transportation became the top culprit of human-generated greenhouse gas emissions (GHG), making up 29% of the greenhouse emissions in the U.S. So using alternative transportation in our own lives is key! (When we say transportation we’re including commercial and noncommercial planes, trains, automobiles, trucks, ships, and boats.)


When you use public transportation, you not only help curb global warming, but your estimated average savings from not using a car could be $10,000 to $15,000 per year, depending on where you live. (Source: American Public Transportation Association.)


Communities that have strong public transportation have contributed to reducing our nation’s yearly carbon footprint by more than 37 million metric tons! An even more environmentally friendly way to travel is by walking or biking. And the cost difference between maintaining a bicycle and maintaining a car is about $8,370.

Always remember to stay safe when walking and biking. For safety tips for kids and adults, check out the resource pages at the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration:
