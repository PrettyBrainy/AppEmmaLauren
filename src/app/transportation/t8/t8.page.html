Sayonara Single-Use

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

Plastic covers every inch of our world. It can even be found in the guts of animals; 90% of our world’s sea birds, more than half of our sea turtles, and even our whales ingest this harmful plastic. Upwards of 180 species have been documented to have ingested some type of plastic. It’s predicted that by 2050 the amount of plastic in our oceans will exceed the amount of fish in our oceans. Plastic clogs the animal’s digestive system and releases chemicals that are extremely harmful and in most cases life threatening. Some of these chemicals are even transferred into food chains as larger animals eat the prey that have already ingested poisonous plastic.


Creating plastic also takes an enormous toll on our environment and on our fossil fuel supply. Up to 8% of the world’s oil goes into the manufacturing of plastics. On top of this, a large amount of pollutants are produced in the manufacturing of plastic. These pollutants are toxic byproducts of plastic production that pollute our air, water, and soil. The plastic problem isn't going away anytime soon either without action from our communities. According to researchers, the volume of plastic production has grown by 9% per year making it more difficult to reverse and solve the issue.

Take the Pledge Sayonara Single-Use

Lead your family with a goal of stopping all use of single-use plastics. You can start right now by making the transition with three items. If it is absolutely necessary to use a single use plastic, try going for a biodegradable option. Otherwise switch to a reusable item;


instead of using a single use coffee cup straight from the shop, use a reusable mug (some locations even offer discounts for environmentally friendly customers).


Do you find yourself buying a lot of plastic water bottles? Switch to a reusable water bottle to save money and prevent plastic pollution.


It may sound like a cliche, but using a metal straw can really help save the turtles by replacing up to 540 harmful plastic straws per year.


When you grocery shop, bring reusable bags to replace the plastic ones stores provide.


Do you like to pack meals? Try using a beeswax cloth to replace your ziplock bags and plastic wrap, glass or food grade silicone instead of tupperware, and stainless steel utensils instead of plastic to pack your lunches.
