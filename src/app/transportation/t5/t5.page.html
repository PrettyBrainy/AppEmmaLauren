Sustainable Flight = Environmental Might

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

Jets and planes use a lot of energy, which means they emit a lot of carbon dioxide. A single flight across the United States can produce the equivalent of about 1 metric ton of carbon dioxide per capita! That alone is 1/16 of the air traveling American’s carbon production for a whole year. Air travel around the globe contributes around 1 gigaton of carbon dioxide per year. Approximately 8% of the world’s greenhouse gas emissions are due to air travel. U.S. citizens fly the most miles, and 1/3 of the world’s air traffic takes place in the United States.


If you do choose to fly, try to take a nonstop flight. Flying nonstop is more sustainable because planes require significantly less energy when cruising than when taking off and landing. When choosing a flight, try not to choose business or first class, which encourages airlines to install more business and first class spaces. Choose coach and make flying more energy efficient because it transports more people with the same amound of fuel! Business class is 2 to 3 times less efficient than an economy seat, and first class is up to 4 times less efficient than flying coach.


When you and your family do fly, consider buying carbon offsets through airlines, such as United Airlines and JetBlue. Here is information to make it happen:
