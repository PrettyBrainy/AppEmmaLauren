Park Your Polluter

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

Want to make a big impact on reducing your carbon footprint? Park your car and drive less! Eighty to 90 percent of the environmental impact a car will make is because of how much fuel it burns and the greenhouse gases it emits. As much as 20 percent of the greenhouse gas emissions in the United States are from automobiles. You can change this because you’re in the driver’s seat.


Extracting petroleum from Earth to make gasoline and diesel for cars disrupts ecosystems, destroying food chains and making their sites uninhabitable for living things. A catastrophe caused by the extraction of petroleum, such as an oil spill, can devastate surrounding wildlife for many years.


With the increased demand for oil and our burning of fossil fuels, petroleum producers are using more risky methods to extract oil, including surface mining and fracking. When we generate air pollution from cars, we also create an immediate danger to our health and the environment by putting smog, carbon dioxide, and other greenhouse gasses into the air.

Take the Pledge Park Your Polluter

Take alternative forms of transportation. Consider riding your bike or walking to the store! Next time you need to travel farther away, use the public transportation schedule in your area and ride the bus with your family!
