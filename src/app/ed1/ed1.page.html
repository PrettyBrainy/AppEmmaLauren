Know More Carbon Emissions

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

The greenhouse effect is the trapping of the sun’s warmth in our lower atmosphere, which is contributed to by greenhouse gases and is the main cause of rising global temperatures. A greenhouse gas is a gas that contributes to the greenhouse effect by absorbing infrared radiation. Carbon dioxide is one such gas, and is defined as a colorless, odorless gas produced by burning carbon and organic compounds and by respiration.

Take the Pledge Know More Carbon Emissions Here is the problem: greenhouse gas emissions.

The Greenhouse Gas Lab is an exciting simulation of atmospheric temperature changes caused by a rise in carbon dioxide (CO2) concentration. Have humans contributed to the rise in CO2? Yes, say scientists. Deforestation and our burning of fossil fuels, for example, expand the greenhouse effect. This is the warming that happens when Earth’s atmosphere traps heat trying to radiate to space from Earth.

This lab simulates the greenhouse effect. What happens in the lab is a tiny representation of what is happening to our planet.

Open the Greenhouse Gas Lab activity sheet! This will guide you as you complete the lab. All of the materials, procedures, and information in the lab are listed in this activity sheet. Remember to be extra careful with the thermometers and to maintain a loose grip so they don’t break and become jagged and sharp.

Experiment like a pro
  • Read through the activity sheet before you get started
  • Make sure your Alka-Seltzer® tablets have not expired
  • Use the same amount of water at the same temperature for all four bottles
  • Double-check that all four bottles are the same distance from the heat lamps
  • Write down all of your observations
  • Ask more questions and investigate on your own!
  • Don’t shake the bottles to encourage the reaction. The tablets will dissolve in the water within the 5-minute waiting period
  • Don’t forget to write down your hypothesis
  • Don’t skip over the pre- and post-lab questions
What does this lab do for me?

This lab explains why it is important to lower your carbon footprint! The first step to lowering your own carbon dioxide output is to know how greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide, are changing our atmosphere.
