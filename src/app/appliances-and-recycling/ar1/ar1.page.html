Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

Though it may not seem like it, most refrigerators pump out dangerous chemicals that emit greenhouse gasses! In many older models of fridges, refrigerants that keep your food cold, such as hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), pose a huge threat to our environment and are also present in many air conditioning units, freezers, and vending machines.


Even today with modern refrigerants and refrigerators, HFCs are 10,000 times more potent than carbon dioxide in depleting the ozone layer! Refrigerants have the potential to become as much as 40% of our greenhouse gas emissions.

Take the Pledge Wait, Donate!

Start a conversation with your family about the dangers of HFC and other refrigerants in our environment. If you have a mini-fridge, donate or recycle it to help reduce the amount of harmful refrigerant emissions your household puts into the environment. Make sure you are doing this safely.


To make sure the method of disposal is safe, visit the Environmental Protection Agency’s FAQ page on safe disposal of refrigerated household appliances. Also, go to the City of Fort Collins website to find out where you can recycle your mini fridge in the area.
