Cold Place, Awesome Space

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

If your refrigerator is located by another appliance or in an area that emits a large amount of heat or in an area that is already hot, it will take more energy to keep things cool, and so will emit more carbon dioxide.


Refrigerators work by comparing the outside temperature to the inside temperature in the freezer and blowing excess cold air from the freezer to the refrigerator. This balancing act means that it is difficult for efficient functioning in a space that can become very hot or experience wide temperature changes in a short amount of time.

Take the Pledge Cold Place, Awesome Space

Look at the location of the fridge in your home. Does this area get especially warm or can temperatures change there very quickly? Would it be possible to move your fridge? If so, find a more efficient space in your home for your fridge and talk with your family about moving the fridge to this location.
