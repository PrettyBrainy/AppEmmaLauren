Recycling Like a Pro

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

The average American throws out more than 7 pounds of garbage every day. This garbage goes into landfills where it is compacted and buried. Biodegradable materials, which make up about two-thirds of landfill waste, release harmful methane gas that has the ability to trap up to 20 times more heat than carbon dioxide!


Landfill development results in the loss of 30 to 300 species per hectare, and that means a decrease in biodiversity. (A sports field is about 1 hectare.) Organic and inorganic materials from landfills dissolve and contaminate local groundwater with heavy metals, ammonia, pathogens, and other toxic organic compounds. Landfills also make soil less fertile, and this threatens and decreases plant growth in the areas in and near landfills.


Recycling, on the other hand, reduces the amount of waste that enters landfills.


There are three steps in the large-scale recycling process: (1) collecting and processing, (2) manufacturing, and (3) buying recycled products. After your recyclables are collected they are sorted, cleaned, and processed into manufacturable materials. They are then sent to manufacturing plants to be converted into products for use by you and me.

Take the Pledge Recycling Like a Pro

Recycling is a simple choice to make. By recycling, the scope of landfill-related problems mentioned above, such as soil and water contamination, methane emissions, and ecosystem damage, are drastically reduced. Take the lead in your family to learn about what can be recycled and the steps you can take to recycle them.

 Set recycling goals for your home!

Discuss the tremendous benefits recycling can have on our environment. Figure out how to effectively recycle in your home and keep your family on track with your recycling goals.
