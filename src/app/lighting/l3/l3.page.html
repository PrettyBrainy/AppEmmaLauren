Come to the Dark Side

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

With more than 20 percent of all electricity use in the United States devoted to lighting, there is a lot that can be done to stop the carbon emissions and cost from lighting. While turning off the lights in our own private spaces is a great way that we have direct control over our carbon emissions, there are rooms in our homes, schools, and workplaces that we share with others. Everyone who uses the lighting in these common areas needs to do so sustainably.


The United States Department of Energy guidelines on lighting efficiency say that if a room is not in use for 15 minutes or more, the lights in that room should be turned off.

Take the Pledge Come to the Dark Side

Take the lead on making your home, school, and workplace energy efficient by turning out lights in common areas and orienting the people with whom you share those spaces (families, friends, coworkers) about why it is important for them to turn out the lights, too.


Read the article by the United States Department of Energy titled, “When to Turn Off Your Lights." Then send it to those with whom you share common areas.

What's next?

Help everyone understand the guidelines and what turning off the lights means in terms of greenhouse gas emissions. Create a reminder to turn out the lights once you leave a room and post it in your common room.
