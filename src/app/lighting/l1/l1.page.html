So Watt? My Lights Are Off!

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

Did you know that something as simple as shutting off a light can slow global warming? By turning off the lights when you leave a room, you can reduce greenhouse gas emissions by approximately .15 pounds per hour. Leave a light on for 3 hours, and you’ve generated carbon dioxide equivalent to driving a gasoline-powered vehicle for one-half mile.


Instead of switching on a light, use natural light alternatives by opening window blinds, using skylights, or working outside when you can. If everyone in the United States were to shut off all lights in their homes for 2 hours every day for one year, we would save more than 5 million kilowatt hours nationwide per year! That’s equivalent to taking 764 passenger cars completely off the road for a year or not burning 20 railcars full of coal. So turn off the lights! It’s good for you and the planet.

Take the Pledge So Watt? My Lights Are Off!

Educate your family about how switching off overhead lights can help fight global warming. Bring your family together to read your electricity bill and calculate your energy savings!


To see how much you can save by turning off your lights, check the wattage rating printed on each of your light bulbs. For each bulb, multiply that number by 0.001 and you will have the amount of electricity in kilowatts you are saving per hour each specific light is switched off. Use these calculations to determine how much money turning off all of the lights in your home for 2 hours a day would save your family. Remember that this also reduces carbon emissions!

 What else can you do?

Before leaving any room, turn off all lights and encourage your family to do the same. You can also talk to your family about purchasing energy-saving controls, including dimmers, motion detectors, and light timers. And don’t forget to use alternative, natural sources of light.
