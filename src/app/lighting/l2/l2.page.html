LED EnLIGHTenment

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

LED lights are a long-lasting, efficient type of light bulb that uses less energy than CFLs (compact fluorescent) and incandescent light (IL) bulbs. Over time they also save you money! LED bulbs utilize thermal management, absorbing and getting rid of the heat they produce, keeping them from burning out and making them last longer than other bulbs.


LEDs are also directional light sources, unlike CFL and IL bulbs, which means they emit light in a specific direction that allows them to use their energy more efficiently and effectively. When we use LED lights, we reduce the amount of greenhouse gasses we emit into the environment.

Take the Pledge LED EnLIGHTenment

Take the lead on making your home more energy efficient! Count the light bulbs in your house, inside and outside: how many are LEDs? Based on the lights you find in your home, start the conversation with your parents about switching to or using more LEDs. Use the energy savings calculator (https://www.epa.gov/energy/greenhouse-gas-equivalencies-calculator) alongside your parents to see how much you could save on energy costs and energy usage by making the LED switch.

Lighting is one of the greatest ways to save — or spend — on home energy. This change will make your home lighting more environmentally sustainable and energy efficient.
