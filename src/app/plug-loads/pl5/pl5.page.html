Unplugging Together

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

Do you have a common area in your office, home, or school where shared hardware and appliances remain plugged in when they are not being used? Where are these common areas in your life? The hardware, electronics, and appliances in common areas use vampire loads to suck up electricity when they are turned off and not being used! Unplugging unused appliances and equipment in these shared areas is a great way to reduce your carbon emissions (not to mention the money spent on electricity) while building a sense of community around climate action.

Take the Pledge Unplugging Together

Make a list of the common areas that you use. Are these shared spaces in your home, study areas at school, or public rooms in local businesses and public buildings, such as the library? For each common area that you listed, identify and write a list of the shared hardware, appliances, and electronics in those areas. Which space contains the most items? In which spaces do you spend the most time?


Pick one space that is most accessible to you right now. Look around and identify every item in this room that runs on electricity. Which ones can be unplugged after every use? Which can be plugged into a power strip, with the strip switched off when not in use? See "10 Things Never to Plug into a Power Strip."

Take this Pledge Together!

After you’ve made your observations, make a plan with the people who share this space with you. Make sure that they understand the importance of unplugging individual devices to save electricity. Talk about how taking action together will prevent pounds of carbon dioxide from entering the atmosphere. Use the Environmental Protection Agency’s Greenhouse Gas Equivalencies Calculator. Take this pledge together!
