Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

A Kill-A-Watt (a pun on the common unit of measure of electrical power) is a tool used to measure the electricity usage of certain appliances and electronic devices. A Kill-A-Watt commonly is used to conduct appliance audits.

Take the Pledge Shocked to Learn

First, think about all the appliances you have in your house. Write a complete list of the specific appliances and electronics that you use daily. How about weekly? Monthly? Then, talk to your parents. What are the appliances they use daily, weekly, and monthly? Add those to your written list. Now you are ready to conduct a DIY At-Home Appliance Efficiency Audit.


Electricity usage measurement tools such as a Kill-A-Watt can be expensive, but there are great, free online tools to use to figure out the rate of electricity usage for different appliances.

Conduct an Audit!

Now you have everything you need to lead your family in an audit! In order to find which appliances in your home “leak” the most power, conduct the appliance audit while appliances are off and not in use.


Look at the resources provided to conduct a DIY audit of as many appliances on your list as possible.
