Ex-Static about my new Power Strip

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

Power strips traditionally are used as a convenient way to expand the number of outlets available in a household, but they also act as a good way to reduce carbon emissions. When a device is turned off but still plugged into a power source, it can actually cause the phantom load phenomena, where the device unknowingly steals electricity from the power source without being turned on. This phantom power drain costs money, wastes electricity, and ups the amount of carbon dioxide we add to Earth’s atmosphere. When you plug your devices into a power strip, you can turn off or unplug the entire strip and not suck all that power from the outlet!

Note: There are things that should not be on a power strip! Appliances that require more energy than a power strip can supply include refrigerators, microwave ovens, conventional ovens and electric ranges, coffee makers, toaster ovens, electric skillets, and waffle irons. For more on when to skip the strip and what to plug directly into an electrical outlet, see "10 Things Never to Plug into a Power Strip" at BobVila.com.
