Wake Up the Sleep Timers

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

The sleep timers on your electronics ensure that your electronics are off when they are not in use, which is a really helpful feature for saving battery power. The more time your electronics are off, the slower their batteries will drain, so you will have to plug them in less, resulting in lower carbon emissions. Although this may sound simple, the lower the number on your sleep timers, the better for you and the planet!

Take the Pledge Wake Up the Sleep Timers

Use the directions provided below to find where you can reset (or set for the first time) the sleep timer in your phone’s settings. After what amount of time is your phone set to sleep or hibernate? Choose the lowest amount of time possible!


You can also set sleep timers on computers and other electronic devices. If you do not have a phone, look for a different appliance in your home on which to set a sleep timer. Do your parents have phones? Guide your parents in lowering their sleep timers.

 Set your Sleep Timers!

Setting iPhone Sleep Timers:
1. Open ‘Settings’.
2. Go to the ‘Display and Brightness’ category.
3. Go to ‘Auto Lock’.
4. Adjust to the time you would prefer your device to wait until turning the screen off when not in use (the lower the amount of time the better).


Setting Mac Sleep Timers:
1. Open 'System Preferences'
2. Go to the 'Energy Saver' category.
3. Go to 'Battery'.
4. Use the slider to adjust to the time you would prefer your device to wait until turning the screen off when not in use (the lower the amount of time the better).


Setting Android Device Sleep Timers:
1. Open ‘Settings’.
2. Under the device category in settings, go to ‘Display’.
3. Go to ‘Sleep’.
4. Adjust to the time you would prefer your device to wait until turning the screen off when not in use (the lower the amount of time the better).


Setting Windows Device Sleep Timers:
1. Open ‘Settings’.
2. Go to the ‘System’ category.
3. Go to ‘Power and Sleep’.
4. Under the ‘Screen’ section, select how much time you want your device to wait until turning the screen off when not in use (the lower the amount of time the better).
5. Under the ‘Sleep’ section, select how much time you want your device to wait until going to sleep when not in use (the lower the amount of time the better).
