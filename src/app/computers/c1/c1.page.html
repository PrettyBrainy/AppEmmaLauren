Dim for the Win

Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

A desktop computer uses approximately 868 kilowatt-hours of electricity per year! That’s the equivalent of driving a car from Denver, CO, to Ottawa, Ontario, Canada. Did you know that one third of this energy is used by computer monitors? Turn down the brightness on your monitor and you can reduce the amount of energy your computer uses.


The difference between 100% and 70% brightness can save up to 20% of your computer monitor’s energy consumption without any sacrifice in its performance. (The savings are 20% for a decrease in brightness of 30% because of different aspects of visuals and interactions. These include keyboard brightness and mouse/keypad use.)

Take the Pledge Dim for the Win

If you have a personal laptop or other home computer, keep its brightness at a maximum of 70%, unless absolutely necessary to set it higher, and encourage family and friends to do the same.
