Let's Learn! Information to Get Started

The energy that is wasted when computers are left on standby accounts for 1% of the world’s total carbon dioxide emissions and 5% to 10% of the electricity used in homes around the world. Change the power management settings on your computer and you can drop the amount of carbon dioxide that you and your household emits. You can also save as much as $100 in energy costs per computer every year!


On computers, there are four main types of computer power management settings. The first type is system standby. By activating system standby you can drop your computer and monitor power down to 1 to 3 watts per computer, and it will wake up in seconds. This can help you save as much as $75 annually on your computer!


The next type is system hibernate, which has all of the benefits of system standby with the included bonus of saving all work done on your computer if a power loss occurs.


The third type is simply turning off your monitor. This can save you as much as $40 per computer and drops the computer and monitor power down to 1 to 3 watts.

 What if I don't own a computer?

If you do not own a computer or share a computer with others in your house, lead a discussion with your family about computer power management using the information in this pledge. The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) recommends placing computers in system standby after 30 minutes of inactivity and placing monitors to sleep after 15 minutes of inactivity.
